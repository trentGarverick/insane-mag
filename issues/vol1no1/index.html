FROM THE EDITOR'S BUNKER

Dear Reader Who Was Probably Already Lying Down,

We had excellent intentions for this issue. We were going to cover infrastructure. We had a whole piece planned about bridges.

Then Trump came back from Beijing with what aides described as "an amazing trade deal" and what the rest of us are calling "a scroll with a red bow that nobody has read." He stepped off Air Force One juggling a gas nozzle, a golden ballroom chandelier, a grocery bag with a receipt longer than a CVS coupon, and a sheaf of Chinese brass band recordings — directly into April's inflation report, which said 3.8%, and the Cleveland Fed's May estimate, which said 4.2%, and the national average gas price, which is currently printing new all-time highs in a tier labeled FREEDOM+ at $9.99.

We tried to find the bridge story. It is still out there somewhere. We gave up.

This issue covers: the Beijing pageant, the inflation receipt, the White House ballroom that Congress is apparently funding with money borrowed from your gas tank, the GOP primary process (which has been simplified to "Did you vote to impeach? Incorrect. Goodbye"), forty-seven drone-related emergencies across three continents, a cruise ship that has added hantavirus to its all-inclusive package, a new Ebola emergency that arrived apologetically at the worst possible time, and — because we cannot stop the world — Bulgaria won Eurovision.

We are adding confetti to this issue. It seemed appropriate. Also unavoidable.

— The Editors

P.S. — The receipt at the bottom of this issue is not a prop. We know you know that. We're sorry.

HE TOURED THE PAGEANTRY ABROAD — AND BROUGHT HOME THE RECEIPTS.

Trump Returns From Beijing Carrying An "Amazing Trade Deal," A Golden Chandelier, And An April CPI Report Saying 3.8% — Cleveland Fed May Estimate: 4.2%

President Trump returned from Beijing this week bearing gifts: an "Amazing Trade Deal" scroll with a red bow that, per aides, has not been read by anyone including the person who tied the bow; a diplomatic relationship described in the President's in-flight statement as "very blorpful"; and approximately seventeen brass band recordings that played on loop for the entire flight home.

He stepped off Air Force One, sources say, directly into the April Consumer Price Index, which had been waiting on the tarmac with a clipboard to note that inflation hit 3.8% in April — and the Cleveland Federal Reserve was already penciling in 4.2% for May, driven primarily by the Iran war premium in oil markets, tariff pass-throughs in consumer goods, and what grocery economists are simply calling "the general atmosphere of this costs more than it did."

The grocery bag photographed on Trump's arm — labeled MAKE AMERICA EXTRA AGAIN — contained, per the manifest: caviar at $199.99/lb, golden steaks at $89.99/lb, a golden ballroom miniature, and a receipt from the Beijing state dinner that several economists examined and described as "aspirational pricing."

The MEGA FLAKES brand is now available at participating grocery retailers for $11.49, down from $11.82 before the current tariff adjustment, which has not been applied yet.
