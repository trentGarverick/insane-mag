CLOSE TO A DEAL!

TRUMP SAYS AN IRAN DEAL IS CLOSE — STRIKES CONFIRM THEY ARE ALSO CLOSE — TO CONTINUING

White House Says Iran Nuclear Deal Is Within Reach As U.S. Strikes Continue In The Region — Peace Dove Fitted For Flak Jacket — Negotiating Table Now Afloat

The White House announced this week that an Iran nuclear deal is close, a characterization that U.S. strike operations in the region confirmed by continuing — because the most effective backdrop for peace negotiations is, apparently, active ordnance.

Trump, seated at a gold negotiating table that is simultaneously afloat in the Strait of Hormuz, shaped like a UFC octagon, and mounted on what appears to be an aircraft carrier deck, is signing the treaty with a fountain pen shaped like a cruise missile. The pen was custom. The treaty has not been initialed by the other side yet. A red button labeled SEND sits within easy reach of his right hand.

Administration aides standing behind the President hold folders. One says EVERYTHING IS FINE. The other says PLAN B: MORE PLANES. Both folders are very full. The aides holding them have the expressions of people who have read the folders.

Across the table, Iranian negotiators are attempting to conduct diplomacy while shielding themselves from debris. A ceramic mug near the lead negotiator reads Deal? Please. The mug is chipped. The negotiators have been at the table for some time.

"We're very close," Trump told reporters from the aircraft carrier deck. "Very, very close. The Iranians want a deal. We want a deal. Everybody wants a deal." A fighter jet took off from the deck behind him at that moment. He did not appear to notice. The fighter jet appeared to be on its way somewhere.

"Everybody wants a deal." — The President, as a fighter jet launched from the deck behind him

FIRE WHEN READY!

U.S. STRIKES CONTINUE DURING PEACE TALKS — SPOKESPERSON DOES NOT TURN AROUND

American Military Operations In The Iran Theater Continue As Diplomatic Negotiations Proceed — Administration Describes Situation As 'A Process' — Window Behind Spokesperson Very Illuminated

The United States continued military strikes in the Iran theater this week while simultaneously pursuing peace negotiations — a dual-track approach that the administration describes as "maintaining leverage" and that the Iranian negotiating team describes as "extremely difficult to sit across from."

At the White House press briefing, a spokesperson stood at a podium beneath a banner reading PEACE TALKS IN PROGRESS and confirmed that talks were "productive and continuing." Behind the spokesperson, through a large window, fighter jets and associated ordnance were visible proceeding in a direction consistent with the theater of operations. The spokesperson did not turn around.

Reporters in the briefing room noted the window. Several raised their hands. The spokesperson acknowledged the questions in the order received and described the situation as "a comprehensive approach to a complex regional dynamic." The window continued being illuminated.

"Both things are happening," the spokesperson confirmed, which was the most accurate statement of the briefing and possibly of the month. Both things were, indeed, happening. The peace talks. The strikes. At the same time. In the same Strait. With the same participants attempting to sign things and also duck.

The peace dove, reached for comment, said she had read the briefing transcript and was "not surprised, but still." Her flak jacket needed re-fitting after last week. She has not given up.

"Both things are happening." — White House spokesperson, beneath a PEACE TALKS banner, window lit behind them

Construction began this week on what sources close to the South Lawn describe as a UFC-style diplomatic engagement venue — a chain-link octagon cage being assembled where garden parties used to be held — confirming what foreign policy observers have been saying for some time: the approach has changed.

The octagon, which is being fitted with corner posts, a canvas floor, and a sound system capable of playing Eye of the Tiger at diplomatic-event volume, represents what the White House describes as "a results-oriented negotiating environment." State Department protocol guides are being updated. The relevant section previously read "conference table." It now reads "confirm weight class before arrival."

Foreign ambassadors who visited the South Lawn this week reported that the construction crew was "very efficient" and that the chain-link fence "makes a specific kind of statement." Several requested clarification on whether attendance at octagon sessions was optional. The clarification has not yet been issued.

Trump, photographed near the construction site in what appeared to be a referee shirt over his suit, described the venue as "beautiful, very strong, the best cage." He did not specify who would be in it first. Diplomatic sources in three countries are currently assuming it is them.

The velvet rope — installed on the VIP seating side — has a clipboard. The clipboard has a list. Several foreign leaders are reportedly checking whether they are on the list. The list has not been shared publicly. The octagon is nearly ready.

OIL MARKETS · STRAIT OF HORMUZ NAVIGATION UPDATE

STRAIT TALK!

OIL TANKERS MASTER THE ART OF THE U-TURN — FROZEN ASSETS REMAIN FROZEN — FINE PRINT MINES EVERYWHERE

Strait Of Hormuz Shipping Traffic Adapts To Peace-Talks-Plus-Strikes Environment — Tanker Captains Developing New Navigation Protocols — Gas Pump Prices Update In Real Time

Oil tanker captains operating in the Strait of Hormuz this week have developed what maritime analysts are calling "an extremely refined U-turn protocol" — a navigation technique that involves assessing the current state of the Iran peace talks, the current state of the Iran strikes, and the current state of the mines labeled FINE PRINT, and then turning around very quickly.

The frozen assets briefcase — which has been floating in the Strait since the sanctions were first imposed and has been photographed by several passing tankers — was recovered briefly this week and confirmed to still contain cash frozen in ice cubes, in what financial analysts describe as "literally accurate" and what the Iranian government describes as "the entire problem, right there, in a briefcase."

Oil markets, which track Strait of Hormuz shipping traffic with the intensity of people who have a lot of money in oil markets, have registered what traders are calling "anxiety." The anxiety has been expressed, as oil market anxiety traditionally is, at the gas pump. The gas pump is updating its price in real time. The time is right now. The price is going up.

A buoy installed near the center of the U-turn zone has been fitted with a scoreboard. The scoreboard reads OIL MARKETS: ANXIOUS. The buoy was not officially installed by any government. No one has claimed the buoy. The buoy is doing its job.

"We are monitoring the situation,"' a tanker captain radioed to port. "We are currently completing our third U-turn of the morning. We are fine. The mines are labeled. We can read the labels." He did not elaborate on what the labels said. We know what the labels say. They say FINE PRINT.

FIELD REPORT · PEACE DOVE STATUS UPDATE

PEACE DOVE: STATUS REPORT

ALIVE. OPERATIONAL. FLAK JACKET CHAFING. OLIVE BRANCH DENTED. MISSION: CONTINUING.

INSANE Checks In With The Strait Of Hormuz Peace Dove — She Is Fine — The Chafing Is Real — She Has Not Given Up And Would Like That On The Record

We are happy to report that the peace dove is alive and operational.

She is tired. The flak jacket, which was fitted last issue and has not been re-fitted despite our offers, chafes at the left wing. The olive branch she is carrying has sustained moderate debris damage and is, in her words, "not looking its best." The Strait is loud. The mines are numerous. The negotiations she is symbolizing have not yet produced a signed document, which means she is in week three of what was described to her as "a short assignment."

When asked for a status on the peace talks, the dove looked at the scoreboard, looked at the explosions, looked at the SEND button visible in the distance on the negotiating table, and said: "Close. I think. It depends on what you mean by close."

When asked about the strikes continuing during the peace talks, she said: "I am aware."

When asked whether she was going to give up, she repositioned the olive branch in her beak, adjusted her helmet, and said: "No." Then she took off again, toward the negotiating table, which was still afloat, still surrounded by ordnance, and still has not produced a signed treaty. She is flying toward it anyway.

We respect her for it. We are rooting for her. The flak jacket re-fitting offer remains open.
